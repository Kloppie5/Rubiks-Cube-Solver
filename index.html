Solving the cross by intuition
It is usually advised to solve the cross on the top of the cube and rotate the cube afterwards, as well as focus on solving a specific color at all times, like white.
Personally, I'd advise to either pick a color at random or already try to look for which color has the most pieces close together.
Simple Edge OLL F RUR'U' F'
Flips 2 to 8, flips 8 to 6, and moves 6 to 2

This gives 4 different cycles, two of which are relevant;



2-Look OLL, Edges
F RUR'U' F' flips 2 to 8, flips 8 to 6, and moves 6 to 2

f RUR'U' f' flips 2 to 8, moves 8 to 4, and flips 4 to 2

These can be combined to solve all the possible edge orientations;
(F RUR'U' F') (f RUR'U' f') solves the dot case
(F RUR'U' F') solves the I case
(f RUR'U' f') solves the L case
Simple Corner OLL
RUR'URU2R'U2
Rotates 1, 3, and 9 clockwise

By looking at the different corners and seeing how many rotations they need, modulo 3, we can rotate all the corners into the correct orientation.
Simple Corner PLL
RUR'U' R'F R2U'R'U' RUR'F'
Swaps 3 and 9, and 4 and 6
This can be used several times to swap the corners around until they are in the correct position relative to each other.
Simple Edge PLL
M2 U' M' U'2 M U' M2
Rotates 4, 6, and 8 clockwise
